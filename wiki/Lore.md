# Lore — Cordite Wars: Six Fronts

> *"The wars of the old world were fought over oil and ideology. Ours are fought over Cordite — because without it, nothing moves, nothing thinks, and nothing survives."*

---

## The World

Cordite Wars is set in a near-future world where civilization depends on a single exotic mineral: **Cordite**. Cordite is a dense, crystalline substance found in deep geological deposits. When processed, it serves as an all-purpose energy carrier — powering everything from infantry rifle charges to anti-gravity platforms to continent-spanning defense networks. Whoever controls Cordite controls the future.

The surface world is scarred by decades of industrial extraction. Forests have given way to strip mines. Rivers run grey with processing runoff. Mountains have been cored from the inside by deep-drilling operations. The factions that now fight each other emerged from this resource pressure — each adapting to the world as they found it, each convinced their path forward is the only viable one.

There is no unified government. There are no international laws that hold. There is only the race to secure the next node, and the will to defend what you've taken.

---

## Cordite: The Resource That Started Everything

Cordite nodes form naturally over geological timescales when certain mineral compounds concentrate under pressure. Each node holds a finite reservoir — typically **10,000 units** before depletion. When a node runs dry, the army that depended on it must move on or collapse.

The Cordite extraction cycle defines the rhythm of every war:

1. **Discover** a new node (via scouting or forward reconnaissance)
2. **Secure** the node against competing factions
3. **Harvest** via specialized harvester vehicles that ferry loads between the node and a Refinery
4. **Process** at the Refinery, converting raw Cordite into usable energy credits
5. **Expand** to new nodes as old ones deplete

The desperate scramble to control Cordite nodes forces every faction to project power far from their home base — which is where most wars are won and lost.

---

## The Cordite Wars

The current conflict — known collectively as **The Cordite Wars** — began when the six major factions' territorial spheres finally collided. Prior to the wars, each faction occupied a distinct region and mined Cordite within their own borders. As surface deposits depleted, they were forced to push outward. The first border clashes were small — a stolen harvester here, a contested node there. Within a generation, those skirmishes became full-scale wars.

The Cordite Wars have no formal start date. Different factions mark different triggering events:

- **Valkyr** counts the war from the day Kragmore mining rigs first encroached on the eastern plateau territories.
- **Kragmore** says it began when Valkyr aircraft bombed a convoy that Kragmore considers to have been in unclaimed territory.
- **Bastion** traces it to the moment Stormrend raided their outer refineries and refused to acknowledge sovereignty claims.
- **Ironmarch** dates it to when Arcloft surveillance networks began intercepting Ironmarch military communications.
- **Stormrend** considers it an ongoing mercenary action that simply never ended.
- **Arcloft** considers everyone else the aggressor and themselves the last line of civilized defense.

What is agreed on: the wars have been running for at least two decades by the time the player takes command, and every faction has lost something irreplaceable.

---

## The Cordite Nexus

The catalyst for the final, decisive campaign is the discovery of the **Cordite Nexus** — a deposit of unprecedented scale, estimated to contain **100,000 units** of raw Cordite. Located in a neutral dead zone between all six faction territories, the Nexus was first detected by deep-scan seismic readings shared (accidentally) by three different factions simultaneously.

Within weeks, all six factions had diverted significant military assets toward the Nexus. The final chapter of the Cordite Wars is not a battle over ideology or territory. It is a race to claim a resource that could sustain a faction's economy for a generation — and starve everyone else.

The Nexus is protected by ancient automated defense turrets — legacy weapons from a civilization that predates every current faction. They attack anything that moves until a player establishes the first Refinery at the site, apparently satisfying some ancient territorial recognition protocol.

## The Six Factions

### Valkyr — The Ascendancy

The **Valkyr Ascendancy** abandoned the scarred earth generations ago, constructing their civilization on airborne carrier platforms and mountain-top aeries. They view ground warfare as barbaric and inefficient. Their economy is built on speed: fast harvesters, fast aircraft, and the ability to strike anywhere on the map before a defense can be organized.

Valkyr culture prizes precision, elegance, and the view from above. Their commander in the Cordite Wars campaign is **Wing Commander Aelara**, a doctrine theorist who turned academic arguments about air supremacy into a practical winning strategy.

*"The sky is sovereign territory. Everything beneath it is just a target."*

---

### Kragmore — The Collective

The **Kragmore Collective** emerged from deep mining colonies, where survival demanded heavy machinery and unshakable collective discipline. They were never the fastest, the most technically advanced, or the most creative. They were simply the most willing to endure — and to endure longer than anyone else.

Kragmore culture values mass over precision, endurance over agility, and the satisfaction of brute-force solutions to problems everyone else considered unsolvable. Their commander is **Warlord Grok**, a veteran armored commander who has never lost a war of attrition.

*"What cannot be outfought can be outweighed."*

---

### Bastion — The Protectorate

The **Bastion Protectorate** believes the world is fundamentally hostile and that survival requires layers of protection. Their engineers are the finest fortification architects alive. Their economy is built for endurance — extracting maximum value from minimal territory, generating passive income that compounds over time.

Bastion does not conquer. It endures. Their commander is **Castellan Mira**, an engineer-turned-commander who built her reputation defending positions considered indefensible.

*"Every inch of ground we hold has been paid for in steel and sweat. We do not yield it lightly."*

---

### Arcloft — The Sovereignty

The **Arcloft Sovereignty** rules from floating citadels ringed with flak batteries and launch bays. Their doctrine pairs aerial dominance with impenetrable ground fortifications — the sky is their blade, the fortress is their shield. They are the only faction that matched Valkyr in the air and Bastion on the ground, though they mastered neither discipline completely.

Their commander is **Watcher Prime Idris**, the head of Arcloft's surveillance network — an intelligence officer turned military commander who prefers to know everything before committing to anything.

*"The sky is our blade. The fortress is our shield. And we see everything."*

---

### Ironmarch — The Compact

The **Ironmarch Compact** fights wars like they build roads — one mile at a time, paved in steel. Their doctrine is methodical advance: establish a position, fortify it, then push to the next. They have never lost a war of attrition and have never won one quickly.

Ironmarch culture values reliability, the weight of steel, and the satisfaction of watching the map slowly fill with your color. Their commander is **Marshal Volkov**, a logistics genius who transformed Ironmarch from a defensive faction into a slow but inexorable offensive force.

*"We do not rush. We arrive."*

---

### Stormrend — The Pact

The **Stormrend Pact** was forged by mercenary companies and breakaway military units who believed the best defense is an overwhelming offense. They don't build empires — they shatter the empires of others. Their doctrine is blitz: hit hard, hit fast, hit everywhere at once, and never stop moving.

Their commander is **Tempest Kael**, a former mercenary captain who rose to lead the entire Pact through sheer ferocity and an almost supernatural sense of when to strike.

*"The only defense worth building is the one you carry in your hands, moving at full speed toward the enemy."*

---

## The Final War

All six campaigns converge on the same moment: **the battle for the Cordite Nexus**. Each faction arrives with different objectives, different entry points, and a different definition of victory. The same events unfold — Kragmore's tank column pushing from the north at minute eight, Valkyr sorties from the east at minute five — but what they mean to each commander is entirely different.

The Cordite Wars do not end with a peace treaty. They end with someone controlling the Nexus and everyone else too exhausted to contest it.

Whether that's you depends on your faction, your strategy, and how well you've learned the strengths and weaknesses of the force in your hands.
