# Factions — Overview

Cordite Wars features **six asymmetric factions**, each built around a distinct military archetype. No faction is objectively superior — they're designed with different strengths, different weaknesses, and different skill curves. Understanding your faction's identity is the first step to winning.

---

## The Three Pillars

Every faction is built from combinations of three strategic pillars:

| Pillar | Fantasy | Strength | Weakness |
|--------|---------|----------|----------|
| **Air** | Speed, precision, vertical control | Mobility, map vision, surgical strikes | Fragile ground, expensive, AA-vulnerable |
| **Ground** | Mass, momentum, overwhelming force | Durability, sustained DPS, area denial | Slow, air-vulnerable, terrain-dependent |
| **Defense** | Control, attrition, economic advantage | Fortifications, economy scaling, area lockdown | Immobile, weak offense, vulnerable to siege |

## Faction Profiles at a Glance

### Valkyr — Air Primary
> *"The sky is sovereign territory. Everything beneath it is just a target."*

**Commander:** Wing Commander Aelara | **Campaign:** Sovereign Skies | **Color:** Blue

Valkyr projects power from above. Their jets and helicopters are the best in the game — fast, lethal, and capable of striking anywhere on the map within seconds. The tradeoff is a paper-thin ground presence that crumbles under sustained assault. Valkyr games are fast and decisive: win in the air early, or lose slowly on the ground.

**Signature Mechanic:** *Sortie System* — Jets launch, strike, and must return to airfields to rearm. Destroy the airfields and you strand the jets.

**Best against:** Kragmore (tanks can't shoot up), Ironmarch (slow armor + no AA)
**Worst against:** Arcloft (best AA in game), Bastion (fortified AA coverage)

---

### Kragmore — Ground Primary
> *"What cannot be outfought can be outweighed."*

**Commander:** Warlord Grok | **Campaign:** Crimson Tide | **Color:** Red

Kragmore fields the heaviest ground force in the game. Their tanks are durable, their infantry versatile, and their late-game Mammoth super-heavy is a match-ending threat. The price is glacial speed, near-zero air capability, and a heavy dependence on flat terrain and good roads.

**Signature Mechanic:** *Horde Protocol* — Ground units grouped together gain stacking damage and armor bonuses. 5 units = +10%, 10 units = +20%, 20+ units = +25%.

**Best against:** Bastion (armor crushes fortifications), Ironmarch (more armor wins the slugfest)
**Worst against:** Valkyr (helpless against sustained air), Stormrend (can't catch fast raiders)

---

### Bastion — Defense Primary
> *"Every inch of ground we hold has been paid for in steel and sweat."*

**Commander:** Castellan Mira | **Campaign:** Iron Bastion | **Color:** Gold/Amber

Bastion builds fortresses, not armies. Their turrets are the best in the game, their walls the thickest, and their economic passive income makes them the strongest late-game faction if left alone. They win by making every attack against them ruinously expensive and then outlasting everyone. Their offense is genuinely terrible.

**Signature Mechanic:** *Fortification Network* — Structures within range of a Command Hub gain +15% HP, +10% damage, and +20% repair speed. The network can be extended with multiple Command Hubs.

**Best against:** Stormrend (blitz breaks on fortified walls), Kragmore (armor runs into kill zones)
**Worst against:** Valkyr (bombers outrange turrets), Ironmarch (siege tanks crack walls methodically)

---

### Arcloft — Air + Defense Hybrid
> *"The sky is our blade. The fortress is our shield."*

**Commander:** Watcher Prime Idris | **Campaign:** Silent Watch | **Color:** Cyan

Arcloft pairs a solid air force with the best anti-air emplacements in the game. They counter every air-primary strategy while maintaining enough aircraft to threaten opponents on the ground. Their weakness is the ground forces between engagements — no heavy armor, no tanks, no siege capability. They win by controlling the airspace and making their base untouchable.

**Signature Mechanic:** *Overwatch Protocol* — Designate up to 3 circular Overwatch Zones on the map. Idle aircraft automatically patrol them, gaining +15% detection range and +10% reaction speed.

**Best against:** Valkyr (outmatched in AA), Stormrend (AA hard-counters their air)
**Worst against:** Kragmore (ground push meets no armor resistance), Bastion (out-turtled by a defense specialist)

---

### Ironmarch — Ground + Defense Hybrid
> *"We do not rush. We arrive."*

**Commander:** Marshal Volkov | **Campaign:** Steel March | **Color:** Green

Ironmarch methodically advances and fortifies. Their unique FOB Truck deploys into a forward operating base anywhere on the map, allowing turrets and basic production far from home. They control territory permanently — every inch they take becomes a fortified position. Their nightmare is aircraft, which can demolish entire columns with impunity.

**Signature Mechanic:** *Forward Operations* — The FOB Truck (unique HeavyVehicle) deploys into a Forward Operating Base that enables forward production, turret construction, and defensive wall building at the front line.

**Best against:** Bastion (slow positional warfare where Ironmarch has more armor), Kragmore (better combined arms)
**Worst against:** Valkyr (air destroys armored columns), Stormrend (speed kites slow tanks)

---

### Stormrend — Air + Ground Offense Hybrid
> *"Hit hard, hit fast, hit everywhere at once, and never stop moving."*

**Commander:** Tempest Kael | **Campaign:** Storm's Fury | **Color:** Purple

Stormrend hits everything simultaneously. Their ground vehicles are the fastest in the game, their aircraft are nearly as good as Valkyr's but cheaper, and their raiding capability is unmatched. They win by keeping every opponent reactive, forcing three-front defenses until something collapses. If they haven't won by minute 15, they probably won't — their defenses are essentially nonexistent.

**Signature Mechanic:** *Blitz Doctrine / Momentum* — A faction-wide gauge (0–100) fills when Stormrend units deal damage. Momentum powers abilities: at 25+, units move faster; at 50+, *Stormbreak* delivers a crushing offensive burst; at 100, *Total Storm* triggers an overwhelming combined assault.

**Best against:** Kragmore (too slow to catch), Ironmarch (outmaneuvered on every lane)
**Worst against:** Bastion (walls absorb the blitz), Arcloft (AA denies the air half of the strategy)
