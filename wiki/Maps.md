# Maps — Official Map Roster

Cordite Wars: Six Fronts ships with **10 hand-crafted maps** covering a range of player counts, biomes, and strategic situations. All maps can also be played on procedurally generated alternatives using the Random Map system.

## Crossroads

**Biome:** Temperate | **Players:** 2–4

> *"A contested valley where ancient trade routes intersect. Two rivers split the map into quadrants, connected by stone bridges. The central island sits at the confluence — whoever controls it controls the war."*

Two rivers divide the map into four quadrants. Stone bridges provide the only ground crossings between sectors. The central island (the river confluence) holds the richest Cordite deposit on the map and is contested by all players.

**Tactical Notes:**
- Bridge control is critical — artillery at bridge crossings can halt an entire army
- The central island is the primary economic objective; do not ignore it
- Naval units can patrol the river lanes and contest crossings
- Arcloft and Valkyr can bypass bridges entirely with their air forces
- Ironmarch and Kragmore excel at holding bridge chokepoints with fortifications

## Iron Ridge

**Biome:** Rocky | **Players:** 2

> *"A brutal 1v1 proving ground carved from ancient stone. A central ridgeline grants artillery supremacy to whoever holds it. Three lanes — north open, center contested bridge, south cliff maze — demand tactical flexibility from both commanders."*

A dedicated 1v1 map with three distinct lanes. The central ridgeline is the strategic high ground — any artillery placed there has extended range against both bases. Both players start at opposite ends with symmetric positions.

**Tactical Notes:**
- Control the ridge early with artillery or lose the economic war
- North lane is open — fast units and flanks work here
- South lane is a cliff maze — infantry and close-quarters units excel
- Center bridge is a predictable chokepoint — expect fighting here constantly
- Best map for learning the Ironmarch armored push strategy

## Highland Pass

**Biome:** Rocky | **Players:** 2–4

> *"Rugged mountain terrain riddled with rocky outcroppings and tight mountain passes. High ground dominates — artillery and line-of-sight make every ridge a strategic prize."*

Multiple elevation tiers with steep passes between them. Artillery has massively extended range from high ground. Line-of-sight blocking terrain rewards defensive placement.

**Tactical Notes:**
- Aerial factions (Valkyr, Arcloft, Stormrend air) ignore all terrain elevation advantages
- Kragmore struggles with slope limits on Heavy Vehicles — stick to valleys and roads
- Ironmarch's FOB Truck shines here — claim a ridge, fortify, push to the next
- Artillery is more powerful here than on any other map

## Badlands Crossing

**Biome:** Desert | **Players:** 2

> *"A vast arid expanse broken by sandstone mesas and dried riverbeds. Two factions clash over a crumbling bridge that spans the only passable route through the heart of the desert."*

A linear 1v1 map bisected by a canyon. The bridge is the only ground crossing. The mesas flanking the bridge offer elevated artillery positions.

**Tactical Notes:**
- The bridge is a funnel — whoever controls the mesa artillery positions wins the ground war
- No water features, so naval units are absent
- Open desert flanks allow fast vehicle raids around the canyon ends
- Stormrend excels here — fast vehicles circle the long way around while holding the bridge

## Dust Bowl

**Biome:** Desert | **Players:** 2–4

> *"An arid wasteland scorched by centuries of war. No rivers run here — only the whisper of sand across ancient ruins. The central oasis is the last freshwater source, and the Cordite beneath it is priceless."*

A featureless expanse except for the central oasis, which contains the highest-value Cordite node. All factions start at the map edges. The oasis is a neutral structure that buffs nearby harvester efficiency.

**Tactical Notes:**
- No water = no naval units on this map
- The oasis node is worth fighting hard for early — establish a Refinery there before anyone else
- Open terrain heavily favors mobile factions (Stormrend, Valkyr, Kragmore with cavalry)
- Bastion and Ironmarch must push across open ground — they will get air-harassed en route

## Ash Wastes

**Biome:** Volcanic | **Players:** 2–4

> *"Ancient volcanic badlands scorched by centuries of eruptions. Lava channels carve the terrain into isolated plateaus, forcing armies through narrow kill-zones. Whoever holds the central caldera controls the flow of battle."*

Lava rivers create impassable terrain that funnels armies into a few crossable paths. The central caldera contains the densest Cordite cluster on the map.

**Tactical Notes:**
- Ground movement is severely channeled — artillery at choke points is devastating
- Flying units completely bypass lava barriers
- Valkyr and Arcloft have a massive positional advantage
- Kragmore's Heavy Vehicles cannot traverse steep volcanic slopes — Heavy Vehicles are nearly useless here
- The caldera is surrounded by the best natural defensive terrain on any map

## Archipelago

**Biome:** Coastal | **Players:** 2–4

> *"Four island fortresses separated by churning waterways. Every assault must cross narrow bridge chokepoints under withering fire. The lighthouse on the central cluster can be seen from every island — and whoever controls it controls the only safe harbour."*

Four main islands connected by narrow bridges. Water surrounds everything. Naval units patrol the straits. The central lighthouse island grants vision of all bridge approaches.

**Tactical Notes:**
- Naval control is mandatory — bridges alone aren't enough
- The lighthouse grants vision advantage; take it early
- Helicopter and jet units bypass bridges but still need bases to land
- Bastion can make each island into an impregnable fortress
- Naval combined arms (surface + submarine) determine who can project force to other islands

## Coral Atoll

**Biome:** Tropical | **Players:** 2–4

> *"A tropical atoll built on centuries of coral growth. A shimmering central lagoon splits land access, forcing all advances through narrow coastal corridors and across shallow reefs. Naval supremacy here is not a luxury — it is a prerequisite for victory."*

The central lagoon is mostly water with coral reef shallows. Naval units dominate the interior. Ground forces are limited to the coastal corridors.

**Tactical Notes:**
- Naval supremacy is mandatory — the interior is inaccessible by ground
- Reef shallows allow certain shallow-draft naval units to pass but block heavy capital ships
- Valkyr's carrier-based operations are at peak effectiveness here
- Kragmore and Ironmarch are at their most disadvantaged — primarily ground factions stuck on narrow corridors

---

## Tidal Flats

**Biome:** Coastal | **Players:** 2–6

> *"A sprawling coastal theatre where jungle meets the sea. Six factions contest a chain of inlets and sandbanks. Naval supremacy and rapid coastal strikes decide victory as much as ground control."*

A large multi-player map where coastal terrain creates alternating land/water zones. All six factions can start simultaneously on this map.

**Tactical Notes:**
- Best 6-player map before Six Fronts
- Jungle terrain provides concealment for infantry and light vehicles
- The sandbank shallows create contested naval zones where lighter craft excel
- Stormrend's coastal raiding capability is at its strongest here
- Designed as a warm-up for the Six Fronts dynamic

## Six Fronts

**Biome:** Mixed | **Players:** 6 (campaign final mission only)

> *"The final battlefield. Six factions converge on the Cordite Nexus — an inexhaustible wellspring of power guarded by ancient automated turrets. Six rivers divide the map into sectors. Sector warfare will determine who earns the right to claim the Nexus — and end the war."*

The largest map in the game. A hexagonal layout with six starting positions arranged around the central **Cordite Nexus** — a 100,000-unit deposit guarded by neutral automated defense turrets. Six rivers separate each sector. Contested territory between starting positions holds smaller Cordite nodes and strategic terrain.

**Starting Positions:**
1. **North:** Kragmore
2. **Northeast:** Stormrend
3. **Southeast:** Valkyr
4. **South:** Arcloft
5. **Southwest:** Bastion
6. **Northwest:** Ironmarch

**The Cordite Nexus:**
- Contains 100,000 Cordite — the largest deposit in the game
- Guarded by ancient neutral auto-turrets until the first Refinery is established there
- First faction to establish a Refinery on the Nexus gains a decisive economic advantage
- Each faction has a different objective for the Nexus based on their campaign narrative

**Tactical Notes:**
- Early diplomacy (implicit — through who you attack first) shapes the entire match
- All six factions will eventually turn toward the Nexus — every expansion is a race
- Kragmore's tank column pushes south at ~minute 8 of game time
- Valkyr sorties from the east at ~minute 5
- The Nexus tipping point typically occurs between minutes 15–25

## Procedurally Generated Maps

The **Random Map** system in Skirmish Lobby generates new maps from seeds. Parameters:

| Parameter | Options |
|-----------|---------|
| Players | 2–6 |
| Biome | Temperate, Desert, Rocky, Coastal, Volcanic, Archipelago |
| Map Size | 64×64 to 200×200 cells |
| Seed | Any integer (same seed = same map) |
| Cordite Nodes per Player | 1–5 |
| Elevation Zones | Low (flat) to High (mountainous) |
| Rivers | On / Off (forced off for Desert/Volcanic) |
| Paths | On / Off (auto-generated roads between starts) |
| Prop Density | 0.0 (barren) to 1.0 (dense) |

See the [Map Making](Map-Making) guide for details on the procedural generation system and the in-game editor.
